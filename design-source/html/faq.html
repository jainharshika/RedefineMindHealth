Questions, answered

Finding the right approach to your mental well-being is important. We've gathered some common inquiries to help you feel comfortable before we begin.

Your first session is primarily about connection. We will discuss your current challenges, your history, and what you hope to achieve. There's no pressure; it's a safe space to explore if our clinical approach aligns with your needs.

We are currently out-of-network for most insurance providers to ensure complete confidentiality and treatment flexibility. However, we provide detailed superbills that you can submit to your insurance company for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

The duration varies deeply from person to person. Some clients find relief and clarity in a few months of focused work, while others prefer ongoing, long-term support as they navigate different seasons of life. We regularly assess progress together.

We offer both. You can visit our boutique clinical space which is designed for calm and comfort, or you can opt for secure, HIPAA-compliant virtual sessions from the comfort of your own home.

We require a 24-hour notice for any cancellations or rescheduling. Sessions cancelled with less than 24 hours notice are subject to the full session fee, as that time was reserved exclusively for you.

As clinical therapists, we do not prescribe medication. However, if we feel medication might be a supportive tool for your journey, we have a trusted network of integrative psychiatrists we collaborate with and can provide referrals.

Yes, confidentiality is the cornerstone of our practice. The only legal exceptions are if there is imminent danger to yourself or others, or suspected abuse of a vulnerable person. We will discuss this fully in our first session.

If you are feeling overwhelmed, stuck in repeating patterns, or simply desiring a deeper understanding of yourself, therapy can be beneficial. It doesn't require a crisis to begin; it is a proactive step toward profound well-being.
